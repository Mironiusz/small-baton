## Systematic Mapping Study

We used a systematic mapping study to identify how AI and ML are applied to solve problems inside HPC systems. The study focused on operational and optimization problems within HPC, including scheduling, resource allocation, workload prediction, performance modeling, anomaly detection, fault prediction, resilience, energy optimization, autotuning, runtime optimization, and code optimization. We excluded studies in which HPC was used only as infrastructure for training AI models, papers on general AI for science without an HPC systems problem, and publications without a clear AI/ML method.

The review protocol included research questions, database selection, search strings, screening criteria, a data extraction form, and a decision log. The search was conducted in Scopus, Web of Science Core Collection, IEEE Xplore, and ACM Digital Library. Search strings were built from three concept blocks: AI/ML terms, HPC terms, and HPC problem terms. Before the full search, the query was validated against a small set of known relevant papers and refined until it retrieved most of them.

The retrieved records were merged and deduplicated by DOI, normalized title, and title with first author and year. Screening was then performed on title and abstract. A paper was included only if it addressed an HPC problem and used AI/ML as the main solution method. Studies published before 2020 were excluded. The initial corpus was extended through backward and forward snowballing with depth 1. Selected papers were then classified by problem area, AI/ML technique, research type, evaluation type, HPC environment, and metrics.

LLMs were used only as an auxiliary tool for article summarization and preliminary completion of the extraction form. All such outputs were manually verified before inclusion in the final dataset.
